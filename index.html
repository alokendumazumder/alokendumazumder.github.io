Hello! I'm a Research Fellow in computer vision and machine learning at the Center for Visual Information Technology (CVIT), IIIT Hyderabad, where I'm jointly advised by Prof. C V Jawahar, Prof. Vineeth Balasubramanian, Prof. Chetan Arora and Dr. Anbumani Subramanian. My current research is centered around solving continual learning problems for semantic segmentation in autonomous driving. I completed my Bachelors of Technology in Computer Science and Engineering in 2020 from Delhi Technological University, India where I was advised by Prof. Rajni Jindal.

In the summer of 2020, I worked on objectionable content detection in OTT videos at IBM Research AI, New Delhi, mentored by Dr. Sameep Mehta and Nishtha Madaan. Prior to this, I have spent an amazing summer 2019, conducting research with Prof. Frederic Jurie and Prof. Alexis Lechervy at Image Team GREYC laboratory, University of Caen Normandy, CNRS, France where I studied generalisation and memorization in neural networks. I was fortunate enough to explore visual wildlife monitoring and domain adaptation for object detection in the wild in my third year of Bachelors mentored by Prof. Saket Anand at IIITD.

Research Interests: I'm interested in studying how deep neural networks can be made to generalize well to novel environments and visual scenes. My areas of research include continual (life-long) learning, domain adaptation, improving generalization in neural networks and visual scene understanding. I’m excited about autonomy, be it autonomous driving or unmanned aerial vehicles. I find geometric deep learning and graph neural networks to be really cool. I'm attracted towards challenging AI applications with a social impact.

I’m always up for interesting collaborations or just random chats on AI (feel free to drop me a message on Linkedin).
